SplitFrame

A "side by side, local vs live" bookmarklet

Setup Usage Caveats

Setup

Drag this guy into your browser's bookmarks toolbar:

SplitFrame (Or, hey!, try it on this page!)

Usage

When clicked, on your site, it will ask for two strings:

Needle Part of the site's development URL that is specific to it and not its live counterpart
Replacement What replaces the aforementioned needle on the live site

Example: A site that is locally tested on http://dev.fubar.com/ but publicly exists as http://www.fubar.com/ would have its needle as dev. and its replacement as www..

The needle and its replacement are saved in your browser and will pre-fill their prompts on SplitFrame's next run.

Caveats

  • The site being used must not disallow being framed.
  • Due to the same origin policy, navigation pushes will usually only work one way. That is, you can drive the live site from the local site but not vice versa.
  • Relatedly, in most cases, the only controllable variable in the secondary frame will be its URL. Which is why, unfortunately, scroll events or other arbitrary calls cannot be mirrored.
  • JS isn't polyfilled; requires a decent browser.
